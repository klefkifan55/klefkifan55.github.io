This involved integrating a local large language model to translate natural language queries into PostgreSQL queries.

1.2 Vision for the Future

1.2.1 Where do I want to be in 1–2 years?

My ambitions lie at the intersection of genomics and computational biology — specifically in bioinformatics and single-cell genomics. Over the next one to two years, I aim to secure an internship or junior position within a research group or biotech company working on genomics, transcriptomics, or precision medicine. Ideally, this would be in a setting where I can contribute to single-cell RNA sequencing (scRNA-seq) analysis pipelines, biomarker discovery, or rare disease genomics — areas that closely align with my current project experience.

My dream role would be as a bioinformatics analyst or research data scientist at a genomics institute or pharmaceutical company, contributing to studies that translate large-scale sequencing data into biological insights — for example in the context of ALS, cancer, or immunology research.

1.2.2 Where do I stand now?

I have a solid foundation in R-based data analysis and reproducible workflows. My experience with the ALS project has given me hands-on exposure to SQL databases, VCF-format genomic data, and integrating LLMs into bioinformatics tools. My periodontitis project built real competency in Bash-based sequencing pipelines and taxonomic analysis.

That said, I am still developing in several areas — particularly in Python (widely used in bioinformatics), deeper statistical modelling, and large-scale pipeline tools such as Snakemake or Nextflow.

1.2.3 Skills I still want to develop

  • scRNA-seq analysis — deepening expertise in Seurat, cell type annotation, trajectory analysis (this is also my free assignment)
  • Python — essential for many bioinformatics tools; I intend to start building this alongside R
  • Workflow managers — Snakemake or Nextflow for scalable, reproducible pipelines
  • Machine learning for genomics — classification and prediction models applied to biological data
  • Scientific writing — preparing results in publication-ready format

The free assignment described in the next chapter is a direct step toward one of these goals: building real, applied competency in single-cell RNA sequencing analysis using Seurat.
